Importance of Choosing a Secure Professional Password

Selecting a professional password is a particularly sensitive issue. On one hand, depending on your company's security policies, this password allows access to remote services like your professional emails. On the other hand, in the event of physical access to your company, a malicious individual could access the network through your account.

These passwords are generally subject to strict policies (multiple characters, uppercase, numbers, etc.). This often leads users to choose a single password to remember and reuse it across many sites. When the company's Information Security Policy (ISP) does not mandate regular password changes, few employees do so on their own. Even if security policy requires regular password changes and prevents the reuse of the previous password, many users will still only rotate between three basic passwords, which weakens security.

However, these passwords can be exposed by breaches on certain sites, leading to the disclosure of passwords associated with an email address. The site https://haveibeenpwned.com/ allows you to check if an address is associated with one or more breaches.

The number of email addresses is very high, so it is necessary to target high-value targets (HVT). For this, it can be useful to look at the nominations of public officials in the official journal (OJ). Indeed, only high-responsibility positions are nominative and published in the OJ. This list, available as an open-source, allows collecting the identities of public officials holding significant responsibilities.

From this list, for each administration, it is possible to query the "Have I Been Pwned" API to check if the officials in question are exposed to breaches. Once the officials are identified, the credentials found in the breaches can be tested to see if their accounts are exposed.

This automated and industrialized process allows testing all the email addresses of all the officials named in the OJ. It illustrates the importance of regularly changing your password.
